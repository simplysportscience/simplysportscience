Careers in the Sport and Active Leisure Industry

How the sport and leisure industry is actually structured — and what it really takes to land, and reflect honestly on, a job within it.

Learning Aims

AExamine the organisation of the sport and active leisure industry
BInvestigate careers in the industry
CExplore recruitment processes
DReflect on own performance in recruitment

Key Terms

Public SectorSport and leisure provision funded and run by government/local authorities.
Private SectorSport and leisure provision run for profit by commercial businesses.
Voluntary SectorSport and leisure provision run by clubs, charities and community groups, often not-for-profit.
Skills AuditA structured review of your own skills, qualifications and experience against a job's requirements.
Personal Development PlanA structured plan setting out steps to close identified skill or experience gaps.

A The Organisation of the Sport and Active Leisure Industry

The sport and active leisure industry is organised across three sectors: the public sector (government and local authority provision, e.g. council-run leisure centres), the private sector (commercial businesses, e.g. private gyms), and the voluntary sector (clubs, charities and community organisations, often reliant on volunteers).

Each sector plays a different role in provision and participation, and understanding this structure — and how participation trends vary across a country or region — is the starting point for identifying realistic career opportunities.

B Careers in the Sport and Active Leisure Industry

Job roles span coaching, fitness instructing, facility management, sports development, and sport business, among many others. Working in the sector also requires awareness of health and safety and employment law, and — for roles working with young people or vulnerable adults — safeguarding responsibilities.

Assignment tip: Research a genuine range of roles across more than one sector (public/private/voluntary) — a Pass-level answer that only researches private gym jobs misses the breadth this learning aim actually requires.

C Recruitment Processes

A skills audit compares your current skills, qualifications and experience against what a specific job role requires, identifying gaps to address. This feeds into the job application process — writing an effective CV and covering letter — and preparing for interviews, including researching the employer and practising responses to likely questions.

D Reflecting on Recruitment Performance

After completing an application and interview process, reflection involves honestly reviewing what went well and what didn't — in the application itself, and in interview performance — then using this to build a realistic personal development plan covering short, medium and long-term steps toward the target career.

Grading Checklist

What Pass, Merit and Distinction actually look like for this unit

1. Explain the structure of the sport and active leisure industry in your country. Pass+
What this looks like:
  • Identify and describe the public, private and voluntary sectors with a real example of each.
  • Show you understand how they differ in purpose and funding, not just that they exist.
2. Explain the skills, qualifications and experience required for a chosen career pathway. Pass+
What this looks like:
  • Research a specific, named job role, not a vague career area.
  • Identify concrete entry requirements — qualifications, experience, key skills — from a real job description or industry source.
3. Analyse your own skills and experience against the requirements of a chosen career pathway. Merit+
What this looks like:
  • Go beyond listing your own skills — directly compare them, point by point, against the job's actual requirements.
  • Identify specific gaps, not just strengths.
4. Evaluate your own suitability for your chosen career pathway, producing a realistic personal development plan. Distinction+
What this looks like:
  • Make an honest, evidenced judgement about your current suitability — not just a list of skills.
  • Ensure your development plan addresses the specific gaps you identified, with realistic short, medium and long-term steps.
