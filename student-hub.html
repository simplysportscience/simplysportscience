How Practice Checks Work
Each practice check is a short set of questions (5–10) on a specific topic. After each answer you get instant feedback — correct answer, explanation and the specification reference. At the end you'll see which areas to revisit. There's no score pressure — it's about finding gaps, not passing tests.

Study Science

How to Revise Sport Science Effectively

Evidence-based revision strategies that work — not just reading your notes over and over.

1

Use Retrieval Practice

Testing yourself is more effective than re-reading. Use Simply Sport Science practice checks after every topic — not just before exams.

2

Space Your Revision

Study a topic, leave it for a few days, then revisit it. Spaced repetition is the most evidence-backed revision method available.

3

Learn the Command Words

Identify, describe and evaluate are not the same thing. Know exactly what each command word in your specification requires — see the command terms guide in your course hub.

4

Use Sporting Examples

Examiners reward application. Every time you learn a concept, practise explaining it in the context of a real sport or athlete — don't just recall definitions.

5

Build a Key Terms Glossary

Write out definitions in your own words — not copied from the A–Z. The act of rewriting forces deeper processing of the meaning.

6

Watch, Then Read

Watch the Simply Sport Science YouTube video for a topic first to get the big picture, then read the lesson page for detail. This dual-coding approach improves retention.

7

Focus on Weak Areas

Use your practice check results to identify weak topics. Spend more time on those than on topics you already know well — it's the fastest way to improve your overall grade.

8

Short Sessions Work Better

Four 30-minute focused sessions are more effective than one two-hour marathon. Use the Simply Sport Science lesson and practice check structure as a natural session unit.
