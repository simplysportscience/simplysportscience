Participation & Barriers

Why interest in a sport isn't the same as actually being able to take part — and what stops people who want to play from playing.

Key Terms

ParticipationInvolvement in sport or physical activity, at any level.
Barrier to ParticipationA factor that limits or prevents someone from taking part in sport.
Socio-economic StatusAn individual's economic and social position, which can affect access to sport.
ProvisionThe facilities, opportunities and support made available for participation.

Factors Affecting Participation

Participation in sport and physical activity is influenced by a wide range of factors, including age, gender, disability, socio-economic status, ethnicity and geographic location. These factors don't act in isolation — an individual's actual level of participation is usually shaped by several overlapping factors at once.

Common Barriers to Participation

  • Cost — membership fees, equipment and travel costs can exclude lower-income individuals and families.
  • Access — limited local facilities, poor transport links, or lack of provision for a specific group (e.g. disability sport).
  • Time — work, caring responsibilities or study can limit available time.
  • Cultural or religious factors — can influence which activities, and which settings, are seen as appropriate.
  • Lack of role models — under-representation of a particular group in a sport can reduce a sense of belonging or aspiration.

Exam tip: "Discuss barriers facing a named group" wants barriers linked specifically to that group's actual circumstances — a generic list of barriers, without being tied to the group named in the question, won't score as highly.

Strategies to Increase Participation

Strategies used to address these barriers include government and national governing body initiatives, targeted programmes for under-represented groups, improved facility access, increased visibility of relevant role models in the media, and strong school sport provision that introduces activities early and affordably.

Practice Check
1. Define a barrier to participation. +
A factor that limits or prevents an individual from taking part in sport or physical activity.
2. Describe two common barriers to participation in sport. +
Model answer points:
  • Cost — membership fees, equipment and travel costs can exclude individuals or families on a lower income.
  • Access — limited local facilities, poor transport links, or a lack of provision for a specific group can prevent participation, even where interest exists.
3. Explain how a lack of visible role models can act as a barrier to participation for a particular group. +
Model answer points:
  • Seeing people from a similar background succeed in a sport can increase an individual's sense that participation is realistic and relevant to them.
  • Where a group is under-represented in a sport's visible role models, individuals from that group may be less likely to see the sport as something "for them," reducing their motivation to take part.
4. Discuss strategies that could be used to increase participation among an under-represented group. +
Model answer points:
  • Targeted programmes specifically designed around the barriers facing that group, rather than a generic, one-size-fits-all approach.
  • Improving physical access, such as facility location, transport links, or appropriate equipment/provision for the specific group.
  • Increasing the visibility of relevant role models, through media coverage or local coaching representation, to strengthen a sense of belonging and aspiration.
  • Reducing cost barriers through subsidised access or free taster sessions, particularly important for lower-income groups.
