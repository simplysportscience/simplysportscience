Large Sports Spectator Events

What it actually takes to plan and safely run a large spectator event — and how to judge, afterward, whether it genuinely succeeded.

Learning Aims

AUnderstand planning and management of large events
BInvestigate safety and security at spectator events
CEvaluate event operations and outcomes

Key Terms

Event ManagementThe planning, coordination and delivery of an event from start to finish.
StakeholderAny individual or organisation with an interest in an event's success.
Crowd ManagementStrategies used to safely manage large numbers of spectators.
Safety CertificateOfficial approval confirming a venue meets required safety standards.

A Planning and Managing Large Events

Planning a large spectator event involves coordinating multiple stakeholders — venue, sponsors, broadcasters, local authorities — alongside logistics, budgeting and a realistic timeline, all of which must come together for the event to run smoothly.

B Safety and Security at Spectator Events

Crowd management, stewarding, emergency planning and — for larger venues — formal safety certification are essential to managing the genuine risks large crowds present, from crushing to emergency evacuation.

Assignment tip: When investigating safety/security at a real event, reference actual crowd management measures used at that specific event, not generic safety principles.

C Evaluating Event Operations and Outcomes

Evaluating a real event means judging its operations against its original objectives — safety record, attendance, financial outcome, stakeholder satisfaction — rather than just describing what happened.

Grading Checklist

What Pass, Merit and Distinction actually look like for this unit

1. Describe the key planning considerations for a large sports spectator event. Pass+
What this looks like:
  • Cover stakeholders, logistics and budgeting.
  • Use a real event as a reference point.
2. Analyse the safety and security measures used at a real large spectator event. Merit+
What this looks like:
  • Use a genuine, named event.
  • Cover crowd management and emergency planning specifically.
3. Evaluate the operations and outcomes of a real event against its original objectives. Distinction+
What this looks like:
  • Make a genuine, evidenced judgement, not just a description of what happened.
  • Cover safety, attendance and financial or stakeholder outcomes.
