Sport and Leisure Facilities Management

What actually keeps a sports facility running well — from day-to-day operations to the financial and quality metrics that reveal whether it's actually succeeding.

Learning Aims

AUnderstand facilities management principles
BInvestigate operational and financial management
CEvaluate quality and performance in facilities

Key Terms

Facilities ManagementThe oversight of a sport or leisure facility's operations, maintenance and staffing.
Operational ManagementDay-to-day running of a facility, e.g. scheduling and staffing.
KPIKey Performance Indicator — a measurable value used to judge success.
Quality AssuranceProcesses used to maintain and check standards within a facility.

A Facilities Management Principles

Managing a sport or leisure facility involves overseeing maintenance, staffing, scheduling and customer service — balancing the practical demands of keeping a facility running safely with delivering a good user experience.

B Operational and Financial Management

Operational management covers day-to-day running — bookings, staffing rotas, maintenance schedules. Financial management covers budgeting, revenue generation (memberships, bookings, secondary spend) and cost control.

Assignment tip: When investigating a real facility, distinguish clearly between operational and financial management in your answer — conflating the two is a common reason marks are lost here.

C Evaluating Quality and Performance

Facilities commonly use KPIs — membership numbers, customer satisfaction scores, utilisation rates — alongside quality assurance processes like audits, to judge whether they're actually performing well and identify areas to improve.

Grading Checklist

What Pass, Merit and Distinction actually look like for this unit

1. Describe the core principles of managing a sport or leisure facility. Pass+
What this looks like:
  • Cover maintenance, staffing and scheduling.
  • Use a real facility example where possible.
2. Analyse the operational and financial management of a real facility. Merit+
What this looks like:
  • Clearly distinguish operational from financial management.
  • Use genuine or realistic detail, not generic description.
3. Evaluate the quality and performance of a real facility using relevant KPIs. Distinction+
What this looks like:
  • Identify specific, relevant KPIs for that facility.
  • Make a genuine, evidenced judgement about performance, not just a description of what KPIs exist.
