About This Course

Cambridge International AS Level Sport & PE (8386)

📐 Specification Structure

Cambridge International AS Level Sport & Physical Education (8386) is organised across four assessment components covering the scientific, tactical and practical dimensions of sport.

  • Component 1 — Anatomy and Physiology
  • Component 2 — Movement Skills and Tactics
  • Component 3 — Sport and Physical Activity in Society
  • Component 4 — Practical Performance (internally assessed)

Assessment Structure

How Cambridge AS Level Sport & PE Is Assessed

Understanding the assessment structure helps you focus your revision in the right areas from the start.

Paper 1 · Written

Anatomy, Physiology & Biomechanics

Structured and extended response questions covering Component 1. Tests knowledge, application and analysis.

  • Short answer and extended response questions
  • Data interpretation and analysis
  • Applied scenario-based questions
  • Tests AO1, AO2 and AO3
Paper 2 · Written

Skills, Tactics & Sport in Society

Covers Component 2 and 3. Tests skill acquisition, motor learning and sociocultural dimensions of sport.

  • Short answer and extended response
  • Case study and scenario-based analysis
  • Discussion and evaluation questions
  • Tests AO1, AO2 and AO3
Component 4 · Practical

Practical Performance (Internal)

Performance in a chosen sport, assessed by the teacher and moderated externally by Cambridge.

  • Performance in a chosen sport or activity
  • Assessment against Cambridge criteria
  • Internally assessed, externally moderated
  • Links theory to practice across all components
Assessment Objectives

What Examiners Are Looking For

Four objectives assessed across all components. Understanding what each requires helps you structure answers.

  • AO1: Recall and state accurate knowledge
  • AO2: Apply knowledge to sport contexts
  • AO3: Analyse, evaluate and discuss critically
  • AO4: Demonstrate practical performance
Cambridge Command Words

Know Exactly What Every Question Is Asking

Cambridge examiners use specific command words. Using the wrong approach is one of the most common reasons students lose marks.

Define
Give the precise meaning of a word, phrase or physical quantity.
Describe
State the points of a topic. Give factual detail without explanation or evaluation.
Explain
Set out purposes or reasons. Make relationships between points clear. Answer why or how.
Identify
Name or otherwise characterise a feature, fact or point clearly and concisely.
Outline
Set out the main points without going into depth or detail. Give a summary.
Analyse
Examine in detail to show meaning, identify elements and the relationships between them.
Evaluate
Judge or calculate the quality, importance or value of something. Reach a conclusion.
Assess
Make an informed judgement about the value of something, backed by evidence.
Discuss
Write about the topic considering different ideas and viewpoints. Include evidence.
Compare
Identify and comment on similarities and differences between two or more things.
Justify
Give reasons for a conclusion, decision or point of view using evidence or logic.
Suggest
Apply your knowledge to put forward a reasoned possibility in an unfamiliar context.
